## 🔍 Key Findings

### 1 · Loyalty Segmentation

- 🔴 **RISK** — 64% of clients sit at the lowest loyalty tier (Jade). The inverted loyalty pyramid indicates the current programme is failing to migrate clients upward, creating elevated churn risk and high acquisition costs relative to lifetime value.
- 🟢 **OPPORTUNITY** — A single Platinum client generates ~$1.15M in average deposits — equivalent to **5 average clients**. Migrating even a modest share of Jade to Silver would yield disproportionate revenue uplift.
- 🔵 **INSIGHT** — Clients with the largest loan portfolios (Asian and African nationalities) remain predominantly at Jade level, revealing a structural disconnect between financial engagement and loyalty recognition.

### 2 · Nationality Analysis

- 🟡 **WARNING** — European clients represent **43.6%** of the portfolio, constituting a significant concentration risk. Adverse developments affecting this cohort would have an outsized impact on overall performance.
- 🟠 **ANOMALY** — Asian clients (25.1% of portfolio, 754 clients) generate a total loan book of ~**$1.9bn** — comparable to the European contribution despite having roughly half the client count. The highest-value segment by individual contribution.
- 🟢 **OPPORTUNITY** — African clients (5.9%, 176 clients) carry an average Business Lending balance of ~**$2.4M per client** — the highest in the portfolio — yet remain largely unrecognised in the loyalty framework.

### 3 · Gender Analysis

- 🔵 **INSIGHT** — Total loan volumes are **identical** across genders at $2.19bn each. Fee structures are uniformly applied — no gender-based pricing differential was identified.
- ✅ **RESOLVED** — The observed fee gap ($488M female vs. $475M male) originates from marginally higher Business Lending volumes among Jade males ($591M vs. $577M), not systemic pricing discrepancy.
- 🟢 **OPPORTUNITY** — Female clients underutilise Credit Cards relative to their loan volumes. A targeted CC campaign with daily-spend rewards presents a direct cross-selling opportunity.
- 🔵 **INSIGHT** — Checking Account balances exceed Saving Account balances across **both genders** — signalling an unmet demand for flexible high-yield savings products.

### 4 · Fee Structure & Product Mix

- 🟡 **WARNING** — 49.2% of clients are on a High Fee structure while 64% are at Jade tier — the combination of lowest loyalty and highest fees amplifies attrition risk.
- 🔴 **RISK** — Credit Card revenue ($9.5M) is critically low against a $4.4bn loan book. Asian clients illustrate this most starkly: $4.3M CC balances against $1.9bn in loans.
- 🔵 **INSIGHT** — Private Bank clients, while the smallest sub-segment by count, account for the highest loan volumes by relationship type ($0.81bn).

### 5 · Platinum Client Behaviour

- 🟠 **ANOMALY** — Platinum clients hold Checking ($94.1M) > Saving ($68.4M). The bank's highest-value segment is parking wealth in low-yield accounts — a direct product gap.
- 🟡 **WARNING** — Foreign Currency utilisation among Platinum clients is only **$9.4M** (10.5% of portfolio FX total) — disproportionately low given their wealth profile.
- 🟢 **OPPORTUNITY** — Average deposit per Platinum client ≈ $1.15M vs. $224K portfolio average. ROI on enhanced Platinum servicing is substantially higher than any other tier.
